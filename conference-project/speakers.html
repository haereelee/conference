SPEAKERS

Bruce Schneier

is an American cryptographer, computer security professional, privacy specialist and writer.

Andrew Blum

is an American author and journalist. He writes about architecture, design, technology, urbanism, art, and travel.

Rebecca Solnit

a writer, historian, and activist. She has written on a variety of subjects, including the environment, politics, place, and art.

Richard Florida

an American urban studies theorist focusing on social and economic theory.

Edward Snowden

is a 31 year old U.S. citizen, former Intelligence Community officer and whistleblower. He revealed a vital public window into the NSA and its international intelligence partners' secret mass surveillance programs and capabilities.

Cindy Cohn

is the Executive Director of Electronic Frontier Foundation. An American civil liberties attorney specializing in Internet law.

Ethan Zuckerman

is director of the Center for Civic Media at MIT, and an Associate Professor of the Practice at the MIT Media Lab. His research focuses on the use of media as a tool of social change, the role of technology in international development, and the use of new media technologies by activists.

Dan Tangherlini

is the President and Founder of SeamlessDocs Federal. Is a former Administrator of the United States General Services Administration.

Tyler Radford

serves as Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team's Executive Director. 12 years leading diverse, people-focused, technology and data-enabled projects across the public, private, nonprofit, and international humanitarian sectors.

Jeffrey Zeldman

is an entrepreneur, web designer, author, podcaster, and speaker on web design.

John Maeda

is an American executive, designer, technologist. His work explores the area where business, design, and technology merge to make space for the "humanist technologist."

Zeynep Tufekei

is a Turkish writer, academic, and techno-sociologist known primarily for her research on the social implications of emerging technologies in the context of politics and corporate responsibility.
